NSW Stage 6 Chemistry Syllabus (2017) - Modules 1-8

Year 11 - Preliminary Course

Foundation modules covering chemical principles and quantitative chemistry

Module 1: Properties and Structure of Matter

Focus: This module explores the structure and properties of matter at the atomic and molecular level, including chemical bonding and the relationship between structure and properties.

Key Inquiry Questions:

  • How are the properties of matter explained?
  • What patterns exist in the properties of elements?
  • How do atoms combine to form compounds and molecules?

  • Lesson 2: The Periodic Table and Periodic Trends
  • Lesson 3: Atomic Mass and Isotopes

  • Lesson 5: Trends in Atomic Radius
  • Lesson 6: Trends in Ionisation Energy
  • Lesson 7: Trends in Electronegativity
  • Lesson 8: Ionic Bonding and Ionic Compounds
  • Lesson 9: Covalent Bonding and Molecular Substances
  • Lesson 10: Metallic Bonding and Properties of Metals
  • Lesson 11: Intermolecular Forces - Dispersion Forces
  • Lesson 12: Intermolecular Forces - Dipole-Dipole and Hydrogen Bonding
  • Lesson 13: Properties of Ionic Compounds

  • Lesson 16: Allotropes - Diamond, Graphite, and Graphene
  • Lesson 17: Nanomaterials and Their Applications
  • Lesson 18: Structure-Property Relationships
  • Lesson 19: Practical: Testing Properties of Different Materials
  • Lesson 20: Module 1 Review and Practice Questions

Focus: This module explores different types of chemical reactions, including redox reactions, and factors that affect reaction rates.

Module 4: Drivers of Reactions

Focus: This module examines the thermodynamic and kinetic factors that drive chemical reactions, including enthalpy, entropy, and Gibbs free energy.

Key Inquiry Questions:

  • How can the rate of a chemical reaction be analysed?
  • What are the energy changes in chemical reactions?
  • How do reactions involving acids and bases demonstrate the importance of chemical equilibrium?

Lessons:

  • Lesson 1: Energy in Chemical Reactions
  • Lesson 2: Exothermic and Endothermic Reactions
  • Lesson 3: Enthalpy and Enthalpy Changes (ΔH)

  • Lesson 13: Relationship Between ΔH, ΔS, and ΔG
  • Lesson 14: Renewable and Non-Renewable Energy Sources
  • Lesson 15: Fossil Fuels and Combustion
  • Lesson 16: Galvanic Cells and Electrochemistry
  • Lesson 17: Batteries and Fuel Cells
  • Lesson 18: Environmental Impact of Energy Production
  • Lesson 19: Practical: Calorimetry Investigation
  • Lesson 20: Module 4 Review and Practice Questions
