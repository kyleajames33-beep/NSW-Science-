# Year 11 Preliminary Biology - Module 1: Cells as the Basis of Life
## Comprehensive Lesson Sequence (20 Lessons)

**Module Overview:**
This module investigates cellular structure and function, biochemistry of life, and the fundamental processes that sustain living organisms. Students develop understanding of cell theory, prokaryotic and eukaryotic cells, biological molecules, cellular respiration, photosynthesis, and cell division.

**Total Duration:** ~30-40 hours of instruction + homework
**Assessment Weighting:** Contributes to Year 11 school-based assessment

---

## Lesson Sequence

### **Sub-topic 1: Cell Theory and Cell Structure (Lessons 1-5)**

- **Duration:** 120-150 min (2 lessons + homework)
- **Focus:** Introduction to cell theory, prokaryotic vs eukaryotic cells, plant vs animal cells
- **Key Content:**
  - Three principles of Cell Theory
  - Historical development (Hooke, Leeuwenhoek, Schleiden, Schwann, Virchow)
  - Prokaryotic cell structure (bacteria, archaea)
  - Eukaryotic cell structure (protists, fungi, plants, animals)
  - Microscopy and resolution
- **Practical Skills:** Magnification calculations
- **Assessment:** 35 marks (10 graph analysis, 15 practice questions, 10 extended response)
- **Status:** JSON template complete and HSC-proof

#### **Lesson 2: The Cell Membrane and Passive Transport**
- **Duration:** 120-150 min (2 lessons + homework)
- **Focus:** Membrane structure and passive transport mechanisms
- **Key Content:**
  - Fluid mosaic model (Singer-Nicolson)
  - Phospholipid bilayer structure
  - Membrane proteins (integral, peripheral, channels, carriers, receptors)
  - Selective permeability
  - Diffusion and factors affecting rate
  - Osmosis and water potential
  - Facilitated diffusion
- **Practical Skills:** Osmosis investigation (potato/beetroot cells), modeling membrane structure
- **BIO11-2:** Planning and conducting osmosis practical
- **Assessment:** 35 marks (osmosis data analysis, membrane structure questions)

#### **Lesson 3: Active Transport and Bulk Movement**
- **Duration:** 120 min (2 lessons)
- **Focus:** Energy-requiring transport mechanisms
- **Key Content:**
  - Active transport vs passive transport
  - Sodium-potassium pump (Na⁺/K⁺-ATPase)
  - ATP as energy currency
  - Endocytosis (phagocytosis, pinocytosis, receptor-mediated)
  - Exocytosis
  - Vesicle formation and fusion
- **Practical Skills:** Analyzing experimental data on transport rates
- **BIO11-5:** Graph analysis of active transport rates at different ATP concentrations
- **Assessment:** 30 marks (transport comparison questions, pump mechanism analysis)

#### **Lesson 4: Cell Organelles - Structure and Function (Part 1)**
- **Duration:** 120 min (2 lessons)
- **Focus:** Endomembrane system and protein synthesis pathway
- **Key Content:**
  - Nucleus structure (nuclear envelope, nucleolus, chromatin)
  - Ribosomes (free vs bound, 70S vs 80S)
  - Rough endoplasmic reticulum (protein synthesis and modification)
  - Smooth endoplasmic reticulum (lipid synthesis, detoxification)
  - Golgi apparatus (modification, sorting, packaging)
  - Lysosomes and digestive enzymes
  - Vesicle transport between organelles
- **Practical Skills:** Electron micrograph interpretation
- **BIO11-4:** Processing secondary source data (EM images)
- **Assessment:** 30 marks (organelle identification, pathway sequencing)

#### **Lesson 5: Cell Organelles - Structure and Function (Part 2)**
- **Duration:** 120 min (2 lessons)
- **Focus:** Energy-transforming organelles and structural components
- **Key Content:**
  - Mitochondria structure (double membrane, cristae, matrix)
  - Chloroplasts structure (thylakoids, grana, stroma)
  - Endosymbiotic theory evidence
  - Vacuoles (plant central vacuole, contractile vacuole)
  - Cytoskeleton (microfilaments, intermediate filaments, microtubules)
  - Centrioles and cell division
  - Cell wall composition (cellulose in plants, peptidoglycan in bacteria)
- **Practical Skills:** Comparing mitochondria abundance in different cell types
- **Assessment:** 35 marks (organelle function comparison, endosymbiotic theory evaluation)

### **Sub-topic 2: Biochemistry - Molecules of Life (Lessons 6-10)**

#### **Lesson 6: Water and Its Biological Significance**
- **Duration:** 90 min (1.5 lessons)
- **Focus:** Properties of water and their biological importance
- **Key Content:**
  - Molecular structure of water (polar molecule)
  - Hydrogen bonding
  - Properties: cohesion, adhesion, high specific heat capacity, solvent properties
  - Biological significance: transport in xylem, thermoregulation, metabolic reactions
  - Hydrophilic vs hydrophobic substances
- **Practical Skills:** Investigating surface tension and capillary action
- **Assessment:** 25 marks (properties application questions)

#### **Lesson 7: Carbohydrates - Structure and Function**
- **Duration:** 120 min (2 lessons)
- **Focus:** Monosaccharides, disaccharides, and polysaccharides
- **Key Content:**
  - Monosaccharides (glucose, fructose, galactose, ribose, deoxyribose)
  - α-glucose vs β-glucose
  - Condensation and hydrolysis reactions
  - Disaccharides (sucrose, lactose, maltose)
  - Polysaccharides: starch (amylose, amylopectin), glycogen, cellulose
  - Structure-function relationships
- **Practical Skills:** Benedict's test, iodine test for starch
- **BIO11-3:** Conducting biochemical tests safely
- **Assessment:** 30 marks (structure drawing, test interpretation, function explanation)

#### **Lesson 8: Lipids - Structure and Function**
- **Duration:** 120 min (2 lessons)
- **Focus:** Triglycerides, phospholipids, and steroids
- **Key Content:**
  - Triglyceride structure (glycerol + 3 fatty acids)
  - Saturated vs unsaturated fatty acids
  - Condensation (esterification) and hydrolysis
  - Phospholipid structure and amphipathic nature
  - Steroids (cholesterol, hormones)
  - Functions: energy storage, insulation, signaling, membrane structure
- **Practical Skills:** Emulsion test for lipids, comparing lipid content in foods
- **Assessment:** 30 marks (structure comparison, function questions)

#### **Lesson 9: Proteins - Structure and Function**
- **Duration:** 150 min (2.5 lessons)
- **Focus:** Amino acids, protein structure levels, and functional diversity
- **Key Content:**
  - Amino acid structure (amine group, carboxyl group, R group)
  - 20 common amino acids
  - Peptide bonds (condensation and hydrolysis)
  - Primary structure (sequence)
  - Secondary structure (α-helix, β-pleated sheet, hydrogen bonds)
  - Tertiary structure (3D folding, bonds: H-bonds, ionic, disulfide, hydrophobic)
  - Quaternary structure (multiple polypeptides, e.g., hemoglobin)
  - Protein functions: enzymes, structural, transport, signaling, antibodies
- **Practical Skills:** Biuret test for proteins, denaturation investigation
- **BIO11-2:** Investigating effect of temperature/pH on egg white
- **Assessment:** 35 marks (structure levels, function matching, denaturation analysis)

#### **Lesson 10: Nucleic Acids - DNA and RNA**
- **Duration:** 120 min (2 lessons)
- **Focus:** Structure and function of DNA and RNA
- **Key Content:**
  - Nucleotide structure (phosphate, pentose sugar, nitrogenous base)
  - Purines (adenine, guanine) vs pyrimidines (cytosine, thymine, uracil)
  - DNA structure: double helix, complementary base pairing, antiparallel strands
  - DNA vs RNA: sugar (deoxyribose vs ribose), bases (T vs U), strands
  - RNA types: mRNA, tRNA, rRNA
  - DNA as genetic material
- **Practical Skills:** Building DNA models, extracting DNA from strawberries
- **Assessment:** 30 marks (structure comparison, base pairing calculations)

### **Sub-topic 4: Cellular Respiration (Lessons 13-15)**

#### **Lesson 13: Introduction to Cellular Respiration**
- **Duration:** 120 min (2 lessons)
- **Focus:** Overview of cellular respiration and glycolysis
- **Key Content:**
  - ATP structure and function (energy currency)
  - Overview: C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂ → 6CO₂ + 6H₂O + ATP
  - Aerobic vs anaerobic respiration
  - Glycolysis: location (cytoplasm), inputs/outputs
  - Glucose → 2 pyruvate + 2 ATP (net) + 2 NADH
  - Phosphorylation and oxidation steps
- **Practical Skills:** Investigating respiration rate in yeast
- **Assessment:** 30 marks (pathway questions, calculation of ATP yield)

#### **Lesson 14: Aerobic Respiration - Krebs Cycle and Electron Transport Chain**
- **Duration:** 150 min (2.5 lessons)
- **Focus:** Complete aerobic respiration pathway
- **Key Content:**
  - Link reaction: pyruvate → acetyl-CoA (in mitochondrial matrix)
  - Krebs cycle (citric acid cycle): location (matrix), inputs/outputs
  - Acetyl-CoA + 3 NAD⁺ + FAD + ADP → 2 CO₂ + 3 NADH + FADH₂ + ATP
  - Electron transport chain: location (inner mitochondrial membrane/cristae)
  - Chemiosmosis and ATP synthase
  - Total ATP yield: ~30-32 ATP per glucose
  - Role of oxygen as final electron acceptor
- **Practical Skills:** Modeling the ETC and ATP production
- **Assessment:** 35 marks (pathway sequencing, ATP accounting, location questions)

#### **Lesson 15: Anaerobic Respiration and Fermentation**
- **Duration:** 90 min (1.5 lessons)
- **Focus:** Respiration without oxygen
- **Key Content:**
  - When anaerobic respiration occurs
  - Lactic acid fermentation (in animals, muscle cells)
  - Glucose → 2 lactate + 2 ATP
  - Alcoholic fermentation (in yeast, some bacteria)
  - Glucose → 2 ethanol + 2 CO₂ + 2 ATP
  - Comparison of ATP yield: aerobic (30-32) vs anaerobic (2)
  - Oxygen debt in muscle cells
  - Industrial applications (brewing, bread-making)
- **Practical Skills:** Yeast fermentation investigation (CO₂ production)
- **BIO11-2:** Investigating fermentation rate at different temperatures
- **Assessment:** 30 marks (fermentation comparison, application questions)

### **Sub-topic 5: Photosynthesis (Lessons 16-17)**

#### **Lesson 16: Photosynthesis - Light-Dependent Reactions**
- **Duration:** 120 min (2 lessons)
- **Focus:** Converting light energy to chemical energy
- **Key Content:**
  - Overview: 6CO₂ + 6H₂O + light energy → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂
  - Chloroplast structure (thylakoids, grana, stroma)
  - Photosynthetic pigments (chlorophyll a, b, carotenoids)
  - Light absorption spectrum vs action spectrum
  - Light-dependent reactions (in thylakoid membranes)
  - Photosystems I and II
  - Photolysis of water
  - Electron transport chain
  - Photophosphorylation (ATP production)
  - NADP⁺ reduction to NADPH
- **Practical Skills:** Chromatography to separate leaf pigments
- **BIO11-3:** Conducting chromatography safely
- **Assessment:** 35 marks (light reactions pathway, pigment analysis, location questions)

#### **Lesson 17: Photosynthesis - Light-Independent Reactions (Calvin Cycle)**
- **Duration:** 120 min (2 lessons)
- **Focus:** Carbon fixation and glucose synthesis
- **Key Content:**
  - Calvin cycle (in stroma)
  - Carbon fixation: RuBP + CO₂ → 3-carbon compounds (RuBisCO enzyme)
  - Reduction phase: using ATP and NADPH from light reactions
  - Regeneration of RuBP
  - Glucose synthesis
  - Factors affecting photosynthesis rate: light intensity, CO₂ concentration, temperature
  - Limiting factors
- **Practical Skills:** Investigating photosynthesis rate (oxygen production in pondweed)
- **BIO11-5:** Analyzing rate graphs with limiting factors
- **Assessment:** 35 marks (Calvin cycle pathway, limiting factor analysis, graph interpretation)

### **Sub-topic 6: Cell Division and Cell Cycle (Lessons 18-20)**

#### **Lesson 18: The Cell Cycle and Mitosis**
- **Duration:** 150 min (2.5 lessons)
- **Focus:** Cell division for growth and repair
- **Key Content:**
  - Cell cycle overview: Interphase (G₁, S, G₂) and M phase
  - Interphase: cell growth, DNA replication, organelle replication
  - Chromatin vs chromosomes
  - Sister chromatids and centromeres
  - Mitosis stages: prophase, metaphase, anaphase, telophase
  - Cytokinesis (in animals vs plants)
  - Significance: growth, repair, asexual reproduction
  - Mitotic index calculation
- **Practical Skills:** Observing mitosis in onion root tip cells (microscopy)
- **BIO11-3:** Using microscopes safely
- **Assessment:** 35 marks (stage identification, mitotic index calculation, significance explanation)

#### **Lesson 19: Cell Cycle Regulation and Cancer**
- **Duration:** 120 min (2 lessons)
- **Focus:** Controlling cell division
- **Key Content:**
  - Cell cycle checkpoints (G₁, G₂, M)
  - Cyclins and cyclin-dependent kinases (CDKs)
  - Tumor suppressor genes (p53, Rb)
  - Proto-oncogenes vs oncogenes
  - Cancer as uncontrolled cell division
  - Benign vs malignant tumors
  - Metastasis
  - Mutagens and carcinogens
  - Cancer treatments: chemotherapy (targeting rapidly dividing cells), radiation
- **Practical Skills:** Analyzing cancer epidemiology data
- **BIO11-4:** Processing secondary data on cancer rates
- **Assessment:** 30 marks (checkpoint explanation, cancer mechanism, treatment analysis)

#### **Lesson 20: Module Review and Depth Study Planning**
- **Duration:** 120 min (2 lessons)
- **Focus:** Consolidating module content and planning investigations
- **Key Content:**
  - Review of all module topics
  - Connections between topics (e.g., ATP in respiration and active transport)
  - Common exam question types and strategies
  - Introduction to depth study options
  - Scientific investigation skills (BIO11-1 to BIO11-6)
- **Practical Skills:** Planning a depth study investigation
- **Assessment:** 50 marks (comprehensive module test: 20 MC, 30 marks short answer/extended response)
