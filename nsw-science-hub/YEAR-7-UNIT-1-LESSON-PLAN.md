**Title**: "Observation vs Inference"
**Subtitle**: "Facts vs Interpretations | Objective Language"
**NSW Outcomes**: SC4-OTU-01, SC4-WS-01, SC4-WS-02
**Duration**: 45-50 minutes
**Total XP**: 500

### Learning Goals
- Distinguish between observations and inferences
- Use objective vs subjective language
- Make evidence-based inferences
- Avoid assumptions in scientific writing

### Stage Breakdown

**Stage 1: Introduction (100 XP)**
- Hook: Mystery image activity (What do you observe vs what do you infer?)
- InfoCard: Definitions of observation and inference
- Quiz: 5 questions identifying observations vs inferences (20 XP each)

**Stage 2: Explore (100 XP)**
- DragDrop: Sort 10 statements into "Observation" or "Inference" (10 XP each)
  - Examples: "The liquid is clear" (observation) vs "The liquid is water" (inference)

**Stage 3: Learn (100 XP)**
- InfoCard: Objective language in science
- InfoCard: How to make evidence-based inferences
- CopyToBook: Key vocabulary and examples

**Stage 4: Practice (150 XP)**
- ChallengeLevels: 4 levels of inference practice
  - Level 1: Simple observations (25 XP, 3 questions)
  - Level 2: Identifying inferences (35 XP, 4 questions)
  - Level 3: Writing objective descriptions (45 XP, 5 questions)
  - Level 4: Evaluating scientific claims (45 XP, 5 questions)

**Stage 5: Summary (50 XP)**
- InfoCard: Why objective observation matters in science
- MasteryChecklist: Self-assessment of skills learned
- Hook resolution: Explain the mystery image objectively

## Progression & Scaffolding

The lesson sequence builds:
- **L1-3**: Foundation (what science is, how to observe, what to ask)
- **L4-5**: Investigation design (variables, fair testing)
- **L6-8**: Practical skills (safety, measurement, accuracy)
- **L9-10**: Putting it together (planning and doing investigations)

Each lesson uses the same color scheme for visual consistency and prepares students for the next concept.
